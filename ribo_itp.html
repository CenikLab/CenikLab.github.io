    1. Purpose
      2. The purpose of this protocol is to detail the RIBOsome profiling via IsoTachoPhoresis (Ribo-ITP) protocol described in Tonn et al. 2021 to maximize execution and reproducibility in other laboratories. Here, we detail all reagents and materials needed, steps to take for experimental setup, and the Ribo-ITP protocol, and general considerations.

    4. Manufacturing Ribo-ITP chips
      1. Mix Sylgard 184 PDMS monomer and curing agent (Ellsworth) at 10:1 (w/w) ratio.
        1. For initial setup of the molds, the PDMS mixture should be of sufficient volume to cover the entire mold (i.e. to cover the glass slide and the attached molds) and reach a depth of at least ~2 cm.
        2. For subsequent experiments after the initial setup, prepare enough of the PDMS mixture to provide ~7 g of PDMS mixture to each two-lane mold.






      2. Degas the PDMS mixture in a desiccator connected to a vacuum chamber for ~30 min or until no visible air bubbles are seen.




      3. Pour the degassed PDMS mixture evenly onto the molds.
        1. For initial setup of the molds, pour the PDMS mixture over the entire surface of the mold (i.e. to cover the glass slide and the attached molds) and reach a depth of at least ~ 2 cm.
        2. For subsequent experiments after the initial setup, pour the PDMS mixture evenly over the exposed surfaces of the chips, ensuring that each gets approximately 7 g of PDMS mixture and reaches a depth of at least ~2 cm.



      4. Degas the PDMS mixture in a desiccator connected to a vacuum chamber for ~30 min or until no visible air bubbles are seen.



      5. Incubate the PDMS molds at 50°C overnight.



      6. After overnight incubation, remove the molds from the incubator and touch the top of the molds with gloved fingers to ensure that the PDMS has solidified and is no longer sticky. If any stickiness or liquid PDMS is noted, return the molds to the 50°C incubator until curing is complete.
      7. Using a razor blade, cut the PDMS slabs from the molds along the border of the chips.
        1. The border is indicated by red below.

        2. Take care to avoid cutting the molds and PDMS slabs in the areas of the channels to maintain their integrity
        3. 3 PDMS slabs (6 lanes total) fit on a 5” x 4” glass slide.
      8. Place the PDMS slabs feature-side up and punch out the five reservoirs using a 5 mm diameter biopsy punch.


        1. The borders of the reservoirs are indicated by red circles below.

        2. Cover channels with tape for storage, or continue to plasma cleaning.
      9. Plasma clean the PDMS slabs and glass slide as follows:
        Important note: The plasma cleaning protocol may need to be optimized for different plasma cleaner setups.
        1. Turn on power of plasma cleaner and vacuum gauge.
        2. Place PDMS channel feature side up and glass slide (4’’ by 3’’; Ted Pella) in chamber (pre-clean the slide and channel with tape).
        3. Make sure the 3-way valve is closed (lever pointing down).
        4. While holding the door shut, turn on the vacuum pump.
        5. Let the vacuum pump down to 0.25 Torr (can take up to 15 min).


        6. Open the 3-way valve to let air flow (lever pointing left). Let the pressure again reach 0.25 Torr.


        7. Turn on the RF power to “HI” and treat the samples for 2 min. Bright violet plasma color should be observed.
        8. Turn off the RF and vacuum pump, and close the 2-way valve (lever pointing down).
        9. Slowly vent the chamber by opening the 3-way valve slightly (lever pointing slightly to the right).
        10. Should hear a hissing sound as the air evacuates. This should be done slowly (takes at least 1 min).
        11. Chamber is fully vented when pressure returns to atmospheric pressure


        12. Open the chamber and bring the plasma-treated surfaces together. You can gently push air bubbles from beneath the PDMS, but it is not necessary to push hard, as this can damage the channels.


      10. Heat the chips at 80°C for a minimum of 15 minutes and up to overnight to enhance bonding.


      11. Cover channels with tape for storage.


  4. Ribo-ITP protocol
    1. Cell preparation: dilution method for cell culture
      2. *Use this method if performing Ribo-ITP with dilutions of K562 cells
      1. Grow K562 cells in RPMI 1640 + 10% FBS (media).
      2. Pellet cells at 300 g for 10 min.
      3. Resuspend cells with 5 mL media.
      4. Wash cells once with 1x phosphate buffered saline (PBS) then resuspend in 1 ml PBS.
      5. Pellet cells in PBS at 300 g for 10 min.
      6. Remove PBS and resuspend pellet in Lysis Buffer Working Solution to get desired cell concentration in a final volume of 5 μL. We used a dilution equivalent to 100 cells in 5 μL of buffer.
      7. To generate ribosome protected fragments, proceed to the section titled “Nuclease digestion for generation of ribosome protected fragments (RPFs) from diluted cell culture lysate”.
    2. Preparation of mouse oocytes and embryos.
      3. *Use this method if performing Ribo-ITP with mouse oocytes or embryos.
      1. Refer to Bill Shawlot’s methods described in Mouse oocyte isolation and the In vitro fertilization (IVF) using CAST/EiJ sperm sections in Single cell quantification of ribosome occupancy in early mouse development.
        1. GV and MII collected from C57BL/6J mice (female).
        2. IVF performed in order to get 1, 2, 4 and 8-cell stage embryos (CAST/EiJ (male) x C57BL/6J).
      2. Oocyte collection performed as done by Borensztein et al in Transcriptome Profiling of Single Mouse Oocytes (2018)
        1. This collection method minimizes the final buffer volume in the starting sample (<1 μL each).
        2. Upon collection, samples are added to low bind 0.2 ml PCR tubes (Axygen) and submerged in liquid nitrogen. Samples are immediately transferred to -80°C for storage until sample processing, which takes place as soon as possible. We have found that the sooner the samples are processed after collection, the better our data. For the mouse samples in the ITP paper, samples were processed within 24 – 48 of collection.
        3. To generate ribosome protected fragments, proceed to the section titled “Nuclease digestion for generation of ribosome protected fragments (RPFs) from mouse oocytes, embryos” section.
    3. PDMS chip preparation for Ribo-ITP experiments
      1. Prepare prepolymer mixes (10% and 5% PPM) according to the recipes in the “buffers” section. Heating at 50°C and vortexing the prepolymer mix is necessary to fully dissolve the urea. Vortex again after addition of VA-086, and cover tubes with foil to reduce light exposure.
      2. Attach PDMS chip to blue light source with tape.
      3. Pretreat the channels of the ITP chip by filling the entire chip with each solution for the indicated amount of time. Empty and dry the channels between each step using a Pasteur pipette tip attached to a vacuum hose and liquid trap.
        1. 1 min of RNaseZap (100% concentrate).
        2. Nuclease-free water rinse.
        3. 1 min of 1 M NaOH.
        4. Nuclease-free water rinse.
        5. 10 min of 10% (w/v) Benzophenone in Acetone.
          1. During benzophenone treatment, prepolymer mixes heated at 50°C for 10 min.
          2. Ensure there are no bubbles present during the treatment; more of the solution can be pipetted into the channel during treatment to remove bubbles.
        6. 1 min of MeOH.
        7. 1 min of 0.1% Triton X-100 in nuclease-free water.


      4. Polyacrylamide stacking
        1. Secure chip to UV light source (turned off) with tape to avoid disturbing the prepolymer mix prior to polymerization.
        2. Fill the 10% polyacrylamide channel with the 10% PPM by dial pipetting into the size-selection/10% PA channel through the elution well.

          1. Dial pipetting was performed by slowly turning the volume knob to the left to reduce the displayed volume in the micropipette.
          2. Ensure that the prepolymer mixtures are being pipetted into the channel by aligning the pipette tip right at the channel entrance.
          3. Take extra care to ensure the 10% PPM does not block branch channel 2 when loading.
        3. Fill the extraction channel/5% polyacrylamide channel with the 5% PPM by dial pipetting through branch channel 2. Ensure the 5% PPM does not block branch channel 1 when loading as this will not prevent the loading of the lysate into the lysate channel.
        4. Expose chip containing the stacked PPM to UV light for one minute, followed by darkness for 30 seconds
        5. Repeat UV exposure two more times for a total three-minute exposure.
        6. Fill all empty channels and reservoirs with LE storage buffer to avoid polyacrylamide dehydration.


      5. While waiting to perform ITP, cover the chips with a box or foil to protect from light. Chips are typically used within 30 min to 6 hours of preparation.
    4. Nuclease digestion for generation of ribosome protected fragments (RPFs) from diluted cell culture lysate.
      5. * Digestion should be timed so that the incubation step ends right before loading it into the lysate channel
      ** When preparing ~6 samples in a day, process three at a time through digestion and ITP, then repeat digestion for the next three samples.
      1. Dilute MNase stock [2000 U/μL] (NEB). Dilute 1:50 in lysis buffer for 100 cell dilution experiments by adding 2 μl MNase stock to 98 μl lysis buffer.
      2. Add 1 μl of the diluted MNase to the lysate, cap the PCR tube, and incubate in thermal cycler for 30 min at 37°C.
      3. Dilute EGTA to 70 mM in lysis buffer and keep on ice.
      4. Add 1 μl of EGTA [70 mM] solution to the lysate to a final concentration of 10 mM.
        1. If working with tubes, vortex samples and spin down.
        2. If working with plates, pipette to mix.
      5. Keep lysate on ice until Ribo-ITP.
    5. Nuclease digestion for generation of ribosome protected fragments (RPFs) from mouse oocytes, embryos.
      1. Add 6 μl of Lysis Buffer Working Solution directly to the frozen droplet and place it on a cold block on ice.
        1. Dilute 2 μl of MNase in 98 μl Lysis Buffer to get the 1:50 dilution. A digestion mix was made by adding 4 μl of the 1:50 MNase to 20 μl Lysis Buffer.
        2. Repeat until all samples have Lysis Buffer. Pipette up and down ~5x to mix and then briefly centrifuge.
      2. Samples are immediately digested at 37°C for 30 min.
      3. After digestion, samples are immediately moved to ice and 1μL of EGTA [70 mM] is added for a final concentration of 10 mM.
      4. Samples are vortexed and spun down, then kept on ice until ITP.
    6. Chip setup
      7. * Keep pluronic solutions (LEp and TEp) and frozen pipette tips on ice to prevent premature gelling.
      1. Move chip to blue light source for ITP and secure with tape.
      2. Remove LE storage buffer from channels immediately prior to use using a Pasteur pipette attached to a vacuum hose and liquid trap.
      3. Pipet 80 μL LEp through LE reservoir 3 with frozen tips so that it fills both LE reservoir 3 and the channel connecting it to the elution well. Frozen tips are required to prevent LEp from turning to gel early.
      4. Add 30 μL LEp to LE reservoir 2 using frozen tips, ensuring contact with the polyacrylamide present in branch channel 2.
      5. Pipet 20 μL running buffer into elution well.
      6. Add 3 μL of fluorescent DNA markers to the digested lysate (7 μL). Prepare a 1:1 mastermix of gel-purified 19ntfl DNA and 35ntfl DNA [1 μM] in advance, aliquot, and store at -80°C.
      7. Remove SDB from 50°C heatblock and add 11μL to the sample, then vortex and spin down.
      8. Load 21 μL volume of the sample mixture was loaded into the lysate channel through branch channel 1 via dial-pipetting.

        1. Take care to avoid bubbles Take care to avoid bubbles.
        2. A small amount of the lysate usually spills over into the TE reservoir. Similarly, once the lysate channel is full, it is ok to load a little excess volume directly into the TE reservoir, near the lysate channel.
      9. Add 30 μL LEp to LE reservoir 1 using frozen tips, ensuring contact with the lysate present in branch channel 1.
      10. Fill the TE reservoir with 70 μL TEp using frozen pipet tips. Care was taken to avoid bubbles near the lysate channel and to ensure good contact with lysate.
      11. Place the negative electrode into the TE reservoir and secure it with tape.
      12. Place the positive electrode into LE reservoir 3 and secure it with tape. Take care to maximize distance between electrodes and LEp or TEp, in order to prevent drying of the pluronic solutions.
